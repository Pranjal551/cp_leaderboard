Score Normalization

Your total score is a weighted average of your performance on Codeforces and LeetCode, normalized across all students on the leaderboard.

1. Raw Scores

  • Codeforces (CF Raw): Sum of the actual ratings of all solved problems.
  • LeetCode (LC Raw): (Easy × 800) + (Medium × 1200) + (Hard × 1600) based on solved problems.

2. Percentile Normalization (0–100)

Your raw score is converted to a percentile rank compared to all other students on the leaderboard:

Norm(x) = (count of students with score ≤ x) / total students × 100
* If your raw score is 0, your normalized score is automatically 0.

3. Weighted Final Score (0–1000)

The two normalized scores are combined using weights (currently 50% each) and scaled by 10 to give the final points:

Final = (0.5 × CF_norm + 0.5 × LC_norm) × 10

Example:

Say there are 5 students on the leaderboard with the following CF raw scores: [24400, 9800, 4700, 700, 0].

  • For a student with a score of 9800: there are 4 students with a score ≤ 9800 (9800, 4700, 700, and 0).
  • Their normalized CF score is: (4 / 5) × 100 = 80.
  • If their normalized LC score is 0, their Final Score is:
    (0.5 × 80 + 0.5 × 0) × 10 = 400 out of 1000.
